# Negotiating Agreements

## Scope
Preparing for and conducting negotiations that reach durable, value-creating
agreements — interests, alternatives (BATNA), the zone of agreement (ZOPA),
concession planning, and tactics. Interest-based, not win-at-all-costs.

## Purpose
Help someone negotiate well: understand what each side truly needs, know their
walk-away, expand value before dividing it, and reach an agreement both sides will
honor — the opposite of positional bargaining that leaves value on the table or
breaks relationships.

## When to use this skill
- "Help me negotiate / prepare for a negotiation / negotiation strategy."
- "What's my BATNA / what should I ask for / how do I approach this deal?"
- Deal, salary, contract, vendor, or internal resource negotiations.

## When NOT to use this skill
- Reviewing contract terms → [reviewing-contracts](../../review/reviewing-contracts/SKILL.md).
- Stakeholder engagement generally → [analyzing-stakeholders](../analyzing-stakeholders/SKILL.md).
- Facilitating a group decision → [facilitating-meetings](../facilitating-meetings/SKILL.md).

## Inputs
- What you want and why (your underlying interests), and the other party's likely interests.
- Your alternatives if no deal (BATNA), constraints, and relationship importance.

## Outputs
- A negotiation plan: your and their interests, BATNA, target/reservation points,
  the likely ZOPA, value-creating options, concession plan, and opening approach.

## Workflow
```
Progress:
- [ ] 1. Clarify your interests (the why behind the what)
- [ ] 2. Determine your BATNA and reservation point
- [ ] 3. Research the other party's interests and likely BATNA
- [ ] 4. Find the ZOPA and value-creating trades
- [ ] 5. Plan targets, concessions, and the opening
- [ ] 6. Negotiate: listen, create value, then claim it
```

**Step 1 — interests, not positions.** A position is "I want $10k"; the interest is
"I need to cover X." Interest-based negotiation finds solutions positions hide.
**Step 2 — BATNA is your power.** Your best alternative to a deal sets your walk-away;
a strong known BATNA lets you negotiate confidently and never accept worse than it.
**Step 4 — expand before dividing:** look for differences in priorities that enable
trades where each side gives what's cheap to them and valuable to the other. **Step 6
— listen first;** most leverage comes from understanding their real interests, and
value-creation precedes value-claiming.

## Principles
1. **Interests over positions.** Solve for the why.
2. **Know your BATNA** — it's your leverage and your walk-away line.
3. **Create value before claiming it** — expand the pie, then divide.
4. **Aim for durable agreements** both sides will honor.
5. **Protect the relationship** when it matters beyond this deal.
6. **Never accept below your reservation point.**

## Decision framework
- **Strong BATNA?** Negotiate assertively; be ready to walk.
- **Weak BATNA?** Improve it before/during, and focus on value-creating trades.
- **Ongoing relationship?** Prioritize fairness and durability over squeezing.
- **One-off, purely distributive?** Anchor thoughtfully; still know your walk-away.
- **Multiple issues?** Trade across them (logrolling) rather than fighting each in isolation.

## Common mistakes
- **Positional bargaining** — haggling over numbers, missing better solutions.
- **No BATNA** — negotiating blind, accepting bad deals or bluffing weakly.
- **Claiming before creating** — dividing a pie you never expanded.
- **Winning the deal, losing the relationship** (or vice versa).
- **Talking more than listening** — missing their real interests.
- **Emotional anchoring** — no reservation point, deciding in the moment.

## Validation checklist
- [ ] Your interests (not just positions) are clear.
- [ ] BATNA and reservation point defined.
- [ ] Other party's interests and likely BATNA researched.
- [ ] ZOPA identified; value-creating trades prepared.
- [ ] Targets, concession plan, and opening set.
- [ ] Relationship priority weighed into the approach.

## Edge cases
- **No ZOPA:** if reservation points don't overlap, no deal is the right outcome — improve BATNA or walk.
- **Power imbalance:** strengthen BATNA, coalition, or reframe around shared interests.
- **Multi-party:** map each party's interests/BATNA; watch for coalitions.
- **Cross-cultural:** norms on directness, concession pace, and relationship differ — adapt.

## Examples
**Input:** "Help me prepare to negotiate a vendor renewal — they proposed a 20% increase."
**Output:** Plan: your interest (predictable cost + continuity), not just "pay less";
BATNA (a competitor quote at current price — strengthens your hand), reservation
point (max +8%); their likely interest (retention, multi-year commitment); ZOPA and
trades (accept a 2-year term for a price freeze — cheap to you, valuable to them);
opening anchored at flat with the competitor quote in hand; walk-away clear.
