# Writing Business Cases

## Scope
Producing the decision document that justifies an investment or initiative:
problem, options, recommendation, economics, risks, and the ask. Integrates
[analyzing-cost-benefit](../analyzing-cost-benefit/SKILL.md),
[building-decision-matrices](../building-decision-matrices/SKILL.md), and
[maintaining-risk-registers](../maintaining-risk-registers/SKILL.md).

## Purpose
Give a decision-maker everything needed to approve (or reject) with confidence — a
rigorous, balanced case that survives scrutiny, not a one-sided pitch that collapses
under the first hard question.

## When to use this skill
- "Write a business case / justify this investment / make the case for X."
- Preparing a funding or approval request for a committee/sponsor.
- Turning analysis into a decision-ready document.

## When NOT to use this skill
- The economics only → [analyzing-cost-benefit](../analyzing-cost-benefit/SKILL.md).
- An external sales proposal → [writing-proposals](../../office/writing-proposals/SKILL.md).
- Reviewing someone's case → [reviewing-business-cases](../../review/reviewing-business-cases/SKILL.md).

## Inputs
- The problem/opportunity and its evidence; the options considered.
- Costs, benefits, and risks (ideally from the CBA and risk register).
- The decision, the approver, and the approval threshold.

## Outputs
- A business case: executive summary, problem, options (incl. do-nothing),
  recommendation, costs/benefits (NPV/ROI/payback), risks + mitigations, benefits
  realization plan, and the specific ask.

## Workflow
```
Progress:
- [ ] 1. Establish the problem/opportunity with evidence
- [ ] 2. Present options considered, including do-nothing
- [ ] 3. Recommend one, with the reasoning
- [ ] 4. Show the economics (CBA: NPV/ROI/payback + sensitivity)
- [ ] 5. Address risks honestly with mitigations
- [ ] 6. Add benefits-realization ownership + measures
- [ ] 7. State the ask; front it with an executive summary
```

**Step 1 — real, evidenced problem.** A case built on an unproven problem fails
review. **Step 2 — genuine options + do-nothing;** a case with one option and two
strawmen reads as predetermined. **Step 4 — economics with sensitivity** (from the
CBA). **Step 5 — honest risk is a strength:** naming risks and mitigations builds
credibility; hiding them destroys it. **Step 6 — benefits realization:** who owns each
benefit and how it's measured, or the benefits are fiction. **Step 7 — the ask** must
be specific (approve X, fund $Y by Z), fronted by an executive summary that leads
with the recommendation.

## Principles
1. **Evidence-based problem** first — no problem, no case.
2. **Genuine options, including do-nothing.**
3. **Balanced and honest** — risks and downsides included.
4. **Economics with sensitivity,** not a single rosy number.
5. **Benefits owned and measurable,** with a realization plan.
6. **A specific, clear ask,** fronted by an exec summary.

## Decision framework
- **Large investment / formal governance?** Full case with detailed CBA and options.
- **Smaller/faster decision?** Lean case: problem, recommendation, cost/benefit, ask.
- **Strategic + hard-to-quantify?** Combine CBA with a decision matrix for non-financial factors.
- **High risk?** Expand the risk section and add staged go/no-go gates.

## Common mistakes
- **Pitch, not a case** — one-sided, no risks, no alternatives.
- **Weak/unproven problem** statement.
- **No do-nothing baseline.**
- **Optimistic single-point financials, no sensitivity.**
- **Benefits with no owner or measure** — unrealizable.
- **No clear ask** or buried recommendation.

## Validation checklist
- [ ] Problem is real and evidenced.
- [ ] Options presented, including do-nothing.
- [ ] Recommendation clear, with reasoning.
- [ ] Economics shown (NPV/ROI/payback) with sensitivity.
- [ ] Risks honest, with mitigations.
- [ ] Benefits owned, measurable, with a realization plan.
- [ ] Specific ask; executive summary leads with it.
- [ ] Would survive [reviewing-business-cases](../../review/reviewing-business-cases/SKILL.md).

## Edge cases
- **Intangible-benefit-heavy:** proxy-measure and be explicit about uncertainty.
- **Mandatory/compliance-driven:** frame as risk-avoidance; still show cost of options.
- **Uncertain estimates:** use ranges/scenarios, not false precision.
- **Political context:** stay factual and balanced; let the evidence carry it.

## Examples
**Input:** "Write a business case for a $500k data-platform investment."
**Output:** Exec summary leading with the recommendation and ask; evidenced problem
(fragmented data costs 3 FTEs/month in manual reporting); four options incl.
do-nothing; recommended option with a CBA (NPV +$1.1M, payback 2.3 yrs, sensitivity
on adoption); honest risks (migration, adoption) with mitigations; benefits owned by
named leads with measures; ask: approve $500k at the June committee.
