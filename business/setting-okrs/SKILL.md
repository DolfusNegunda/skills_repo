# Setting OKRs

## Scope
Defining Objectives (qualitative, ambitious direction) and Key Results
(quantitative, outcome-based measures of progress), and aligning them across levels.
KPI definition mechanics live in [defining-kpis](../defining-kpis/SKILL.md); roadmaps
in [building-roadmaps](../building-roadmaps/SKILL.md).

## Purpose
Focus and align an organization on a few ambitious goals with unambiguous measures of
success — avoiding the common failure of OKRs that are just a task list with dates.

## When to use this skill
- "Set OKRs / write objectives and key results / define quarterly goals."
- Aligning teams around a small set of measurable priorities.
- Fixing OKRs that are really output/task lists.

## When NOT to use this skill
- Ongoing operational metrics → [defining-kpis](../defining-kpis/SKILL.md).
- Time-sequenced initiative planning → [building-roadmaps](../building-roadmaps/SKILL.md).
- Individual task tracking → a task/project tool.

## Inputs
- The mission/strategy the OKRs serve and the level (company/team/individual).
- The period (usually a quarter) and current baselines.

## Outputs
- A focused OKR set: 1–3 objectives, each with 2–5 measurable, outcome-based key
  results, aligned to the level above and with a baseline→target for each KR.

## Workflow
```
Progress:
- [ ] 1. Anchor to mission/strategy and the level above
- [ ] 2. Write 1-3 objectives: qualitative, ambitious, inspiring
- [ ] 3. Write 2-5 key results per objective: measurable outcomes
- [ ] 4. Check KRs are outcomes, not tasks/outputs
- [ ] 5. Set ambition (stretch) and baselines/targets
- [ ] 6. Align across teams; confirm focus (few, not many)
```

**Step 2 — objectives inspire and direct** ("Delight new users so they stay") — not
metrics themselves. **Step 3–4 — key results are outcomes, the #1 pitfall.** "Launch
feature X" is a task (output); "increase 30-day retention from 40% to 55%" is an
outcome. If a KR is done by shipping something regardless of effect, it's an output —
rewrite it as the effect. **Step 5 — stretch:** good OKRs are ambitious (~70%
achievement is success); if you always hit 100%, they're too easy.

## Principles
1. **Objectives inspire; key results measure.**
2. **Key results are outcomes, not tasks/outputs.**
3. **Few and focused** — 1–3 objectives, 2–5 KRs each.
4. **Ambitious (stretch),** not sandbagged.
5. **Aligned** up and across; a team's OKRs support the level above.

## Decision framework
- **Is a KR "done" by shipping regardless of impact?** It's an output — rewrite as outcome.
- **Would 100% be routine?** Not ambitious enough.
- **More than 3 objectives?** Cut — OKRs are about focus.
- **Committed/must-deliver work?** Track as a commitment, not a stretch OKR.

## Common mistakes
- **Task-list KRs** ("do X, launch Y") instead of measurable outcomes.
- **Too many objectives** — no focus, no alignment.
- **Sandbagged targets** guaranteed to hit — no stretch.
- **Objectives that are just metrics** with no inspiring direction.
- **No alignment** — team OKRs disconnected from company OKRs.
- **Set and forgotten** — no check-ins during the period.

## Validation checklist
- [ ] 1–3 objectives, each qualitative, ambitious, and clear.
- [ ] 2–5 key results per objective, each measurable.
- [ ] Every KR is an outcome, not a task/output.
- [ ] Baselines and targets set; ambition is stretch-level.
- [ ] Aligned to the level above and across dependent teams.
- [ ] A check-in cadence for scoring progress exists.

## Edge cases
- **Committed vs. aspirational OKRs:** separate must-hit commitments from stretch bets.
- **Hard-to-measure objectives:** use the best available outcome proxy; note its limits.
- **Early/uncertain quarters:** fewer, more exploratory KRs are fine.
- **Individual OKRs:** keep them outcome-based; avoid turning them into a performance-review checklist.

## Examples
**Input:** "Set Q3 OKRs for our onboarding team."
**Output:** Objective: "New users reach value fast and stick." KRs: (1) 30-day
retention 40%→55%; (2) median time-to-first-value 3 days→1 day; (3) onboarding CSAT
7.2→8.5. Each an outcome with baseline→target, stretch-level, aligned to the company
retention objective — with "launch new onboarding flow" noted as the enabling work,
not a KR.

## Automation opportunities
- Roll team OKRs up to company OKRs for alignment visibility.
- Auto-score KR progress from the underlying metrics each check-in.
