# Running SWOT Analysis

## Scope
Structured assessment of internal Strengths/Weaknesses and external Opportunities/
Threats, converted into strategy via TOWS. Not a generic bullet exercise — the value
is in the strategic actions it yields. Feeds business cases and strategy decisions.

## Purpose
Move beyond four lists to actual strategy: ground each factor in evidence, then use
TOWS to turn the combinations into concrete moves (use strengths on opportunities,
fix weaknesses against threats).

## When to use this skill
- "Do a SWOT / analyze our strengths and weaknesses / assess strategic position."
- Evaluating a business, product, team, or option strategically.
- Framing input to a strategy or business case.

## When NOT to use this skill
- Comparing defined options → [building-decision-matrices](../building-decision-matrices/SKILL.md).
- Financial justification → [analyzing-cost-benefit](../analyzing-cost-benefit/SKILL.md).
- Competitor deep-dive → [analyzing-competitors](../../research/analyzing-competitors/SKILL.md).

## Inputs
- The subject (company, product, team, initiative) and the strategic question.
- Internal data (capabilities, performance) and external context (market, competition).

## Outputs
- An evidenced SWOT (internal S/W, external O/T) and a TOWS matrix producing SO, WO,
  ST, WT strategies — a shortlist of strategic actions.

## Workflow
```
Progress:
- [ ] 1. Frame the strategic question the SWOT serves
- [ ] 2. Strengths & weaknesses — internal, evidenced, honest
- [ ] 3. Opportunities & threats — external, specific
- [ ] 4. Prioritize the factors that actually matter
- [ ] 5. TOWS: combine into SO/WO/ST/WT strategies
- [ ] 6. Select actionable strategies
```

**Step 2–3 — internal vs external, with evidence.** Strengths/weaknesses are
internal and controllable; opportunities/threats are external. Every factor needs
evidence, not opinion ("strong brand" → "NPS 62, #1 aided awareness"). **Step 5 —
TOWS is where SWOT becomes strategy:** **SO** (use strengths to seize
opportunities), **WO** (fix weaknesses to pursue opportunities), **ST** (use
strengths to counter threats), **WT** (minimize weaknesses and avoid threats). Four
lists with no TOWS is a wasted exercise.

## Principles
1. **Evidence, not adjectives.** Every factor is backed.
2. **Internal (S/W) vs. external (O/T)** — don't confuse them.
3. **TOWS turns lists into strategy** — the actual point.
4. **Prioritize** — a few decisive factors beat 40 bullets.
5. **Honest weaknesses/threats** — a flattering SWOT is useless.

## Decision framework
- **Strong position + open market?** Lead with SO (invest/grow).
- **Good opportunities but internal gaps?** WO (build/partner to capture).
- **Strong but threatened?** ST (defend with strengths).
- **Weak and threatened?** WT (mitigate/exit/retrench).

## Common mistakes
- **Generic bullets** with no evidence ("good team", "market is competitive").
- **Confusing internal and external** (a market trend listed as a strength).
- **Stopping at four lists** — no TOWS, no strategy.
- **Over-long lists** with no prioritization.
- **Optimism bias** — thin weaknesses/threats.
- **No link to a decision.**

## Validation checklist
- [ ] The strategic question is stated.
- [ ] S/W are internal, O/T external; not confused.
- [ ] Every factor is evidenced.
- [ ] Factors prioritized to the decisive few.
- [ ] TOWS produces SO/WO/ST/WT strategies.
- [ ] A shortlist of actionable strategies results.

## Edge cases
- **Personal/team SWOT:** same discipline; keep candid weaknesses confidential.
- **Rapidly changing markets:** O/T date quickly — timestamp and revisit.
- **Group facilitation:** guard against groupthink; solicit dissent on weaknesses/threats.
- **Comparing options:** SWOT frames each, but use a decision matrix to choose.

## Examples
**Input:** "SWOT for our SaaS entering a new region."
**Output:** Evidenced SWOT (S: proven product, 99.9% uptime; W: no local support; O:
underserved mid-market; T: entrenched local incumbent) → TOWS: SO = lead with
reliability messaging to the underserved segment; WO = partner for local support to
capture mid-market; ST = SLA guarantees to blunt the incumbent; WT = phase entry to
limit exposure — yielding four concrete strategies.
