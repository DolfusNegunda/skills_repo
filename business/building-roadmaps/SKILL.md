# Building Roadmaps

## Scope
Creating roadmaps that communicate *where we're going and why* over time — product,
strategy, technology, or program. Emphasis on outcomes and themes over dated feature
lists. Detailed execution is [planning-projects](../planning-projects/SKILL.md).

## Purpose
Align stakeholders on direction and priorities without over-committing to false
precision — a roadmap communicates intent and sequence, it is not a contract of
exact dates.

## When to use this skill
- "Create / build a roadmap (product, strategy, tech, program)."
- "Show what we're doing and roughly when" across quarters.
- Aligning stakeholders on priorities and sequence.

## When NOT to use this skill
- Detailed execution schedule → [planning-projects](../planning-projects/SKILL.md).
- Goal-setting mechanics → [setting-okrs](../setting-okrs/SKILL.md).
- Justifying a single initiative → [writing-business-cases](../writing-business-cases/SKILL.md).

## Inputs
- Strategy/goals the roadmap serves; the audience (exec, team, customer).
- Candidate initiatives, their value and effort, dependencies, and constraints.
- Time horizon and how much certainty exists near vs. far.

## Outputs
- A roadmap organized by outcome/theme across time horizons (now/next/later or
  quarters), with the rationale for sequence and priorities.

## Workflow
```
Progress:
- [ ] 1. Anchor to strategy: what outcomes must this advance?
- [ ] 2. Group initiatives into themes tied to those outcomes
- [ ] 3. Prioritize by value vs. effort and dependencies
- [ ] 4. Sequence across horizons (now / next / later)
- [ ] 5. Set confidence: precise near-term, directional long-term
- [ ] 6. Tailor the view to the audience; state assumptions
```

**Step 1–2 — outcomes over features.** Organize around goals ("reduce churn") and
themes, not a list of features with dates. This keeps the roadmap meaningful when
specifics change. **Step 5 — honest certainty:** near-term can be concrete; long-term
should be directional (now/next/later), not fake-precise quarter-by-quarter forever.
Over-committing distant dates destroys trust when they slip.

## Principles
1. **Outcomes and themes,** not a feature calendar.
2. **Certainty decays with time** — precise soon, directional later.
3. **Prioritize explicitly** (value vs. effort, dependencies).
4. **Tailor to the audience** — exec, team, and customer views differ.
5. **A roadmap is intent, not a contract** — say so, manage expectations.

## Decision framework
- **Audience = executives?** Themes + outcomes + rough horizons; minimal detail.
- **Audience = delivery team?** More granularity near-term; link to plans.
- **Audience = customers?** Directional themes only; avoid committing dates.
- **High uncertainty?** Now/Next/Later over dated quarters.

## Common mistakes
- **Feature-list-with-dates** masquerading as strategy.
- **Over-committing far-future dates** — guaranteed to erode trust.
- **No prioritization rationale** — looks arbitrary.
- **One roadmap for all audiences** — too much or too little for each.
- **Disconnected from strategy** — initiatives with no outcome link.
- **Never updated** — a roadmap is living.

## Validation checklist
- [ ] Every item ties to a strategic outcome/theme.
- [ ] Prioritization rationale (value/effort/dependencies) is clear.
- [ ] Time horizons reflect honest certainty (precise near, directional far).
- [ ] The view fits its audience.
- [ ] Assumptions and "intent not commitment" stated.
- [ ] A cadence for revisiting the roadmap exists.

## Edge cases
- **Fast-changing markets:** shorter horizons, now/next/later, frequent refresh.
- **Committed customer promises:** separate contractual commitments from directional intent.
- **Dependency-heavy programs:** show cross-stream dependencies explicitly.
- **Competing stakeholder priorities:** make the prioritization criteria visible to defend trade-offs.

## Examples
**Input:** "Build a 2026 product roadmap for leadership."
**Output:** Themed roadmap — Now: reduce onboarding friction (churn outcome); Next:
self-serve analytics (expansion outcome); Later: platform API (ecosystem outcome) —
each tied to a goal, prioritized by value/effort, with near-term items concrete and
later ones directional, labeled "intent, not committed dates".

## Automation opportunities
- Generate audience-specific views (exec/team/customer) from one source of initiatives.
- Link roadmap themes to OKRs and project plans so status rolls up automatically.
