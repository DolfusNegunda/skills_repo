# Facilitating Meetings

## Scope
Designing and running meetings and workshops that reach outcomes — purpose and
agenda design, participant selection, in-session facilitation, decision techniques,
and follow-up. Formal minutes are
[recording-meeting-minutes](../../office/recording-meeting-minutes/SKILL.md).

## Purpose
Make meetings earn their cost: every meeting has a clear purpose and outcome, the
right people, active facilitation that includes everyone, and decisions/actions
captured — instead of the default status-theatre that wastes collective time.

## When to use this skill
- "Plan / facilitate a meeting or workshop / run a session."
- "Make our meetings more productive."
- Decision, brainstorming, planning, retrospective, or alignment sessions.

## When NOT to use this skill
- Recording formal minutes → [recording-meeting-minutes](../../office/recording-meeting-minutes/SKILL.md).
- Async collaboration setup → [collaborating-in-teams](../../office/collaborating-in-teams/SKILL.md).
- The decision framework itself → [building-decision-matrices](../building-decision-matrices/SKILL.md).

## Inputs
- The meeting's purpose and desired outcome; the decision or output needed.
- Candidate participants, time available, and in-person/remote/hybrid format.

## Outputs
- A meeting design: objective, agenda with timeboxes and formats, participant list
  with roles, facilitation plan, and a follow-up (decisions + actions) structure.

## Workflow
```
Progress:
- [ ] 1. Define the purpose and the specific outcome
- [ ] 2. Decide if a meeting is even needed (vs. async)
- [ ] 3. Invite only who's needed; assign roles
- [ ] 4. Build a timeboxed agenda with a format per item
- [ ] 5. Facilitate: open clearly, include everyone, drive to decisions
- [ ] 6. Close with decisions, owners, and next steps; follow up
```

**Step 2 — should this be a meeting?** If there's no discussion or decision, it's an
email. Protect people's time. **Step 3 — right people, roles:** facilitator, decider,
note-taker, contributors; more attendees ≠ better. **Step 4 — agenda as outcomes:**
each item has a purpose (decide/discuss/inform), a timebox, and a format. **Step 5 —
active facilitation:** draw out quiet voices, curb dominators, park tangents, and push
to actual decisions using a clear method (consent, vote, decider-decides). **Step 6 —
outcomes or it didn't count:** end with decisions, owners, and dates.

## Principles
1. **Purpose and outcome first;** no meeting without them.
2. **Only necessary people,** with defined roles.
3. **Agenda = outcomes + timeboxes,** not topics.
4. **Facilitate actively** — inclusion and progress, not just moderation.
5. **Drive to decisions** with an explicit method.
6. **Close with owned actions;** follow up.

## Decision framework
- **No decision/discussion needed?** Cancel; send an update instead.
- **Decision meeting?** Name the decider and the method up front.
- **Divergent input needed?** Brainstorm formats (silent write, round-robin) before converging.
- **Hybrid?** Assign someone to actively include remote participants.

## Common mistakes
- **Meeting with no purpose/outcome** — status theatre.
- **Too many attendees** — diffuse and slow.
- **Topic agendas** with no timeboxes or intended outcome.
- **Passive moderation** — loudest voices dominate, quiet ones lost.
- **Ending with no decisions or owners.**
- **No follow-up** — actions evaporate.

## Validation checklist
- [ ] Clear purpose and a specific desired outcome.
- [ ] Meeting justified vs. async.
- [ ] Only necessary participants; roles assigned.
- [ ] Agenda has per-item purpose, format, and timebox.
- [ ] Facilitation plan for inclusion and decision-making.
- [ ] Decisions, owners, and next steps captured and shared.

## Edge cases
- **Recurring meetings:** periodically re-justify; kill or shorten stale ones.
- **Conflict-heavy topics:** set norms, use structured turn-taking, separate people from problem.
- **Large workshops:** break into small groups; use facilitation structures (1-2-4-all).
- **Remote/hybrid:** shorter, more interaction, explicit remote inclusion, camera/mute norms.

## Examples
**Input:** "Facilitate a 90-min workshop to prioritize our 2026 initiatives."
**Output:** Design: objective (agree a ranked top-5), 8 participants with roles,
agenda — frame (10m) → silent generation (15m) → cluster & discuss (30m) → weighted
dot-vote (20m) → confirm + owners (15m); facilitation plan to include everyone and
timebox debate; close with the ranked list, owners, and a follow-up note.

## Automation opportunities
- Send agenda + pre-reads automatically; convert outcomes into tracked actions.
- Feed decisions into [recording-meeting-minutes](../../office/recording-meeting-minutes/SKILL.md) for formal records.
