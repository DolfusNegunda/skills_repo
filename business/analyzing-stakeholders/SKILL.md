# Analyzing Stakeholders

## Scope
Identifying everyone affected by or influencing an initiative, assessing their
power/interest/attitude, and planning how to engage each. Feeds communications,
change management, and project planning.

## Purpose
Prevent the classic failure — a technically sound initiative sunk by an unmanaged
stakeholder — by knowing who matters, what they need, and how to engage them before
resistance forms.

## When to use this skill
- "Do a stakeholder analysis / map stakeholders."
- "Who do we need to engage / manage / get buy-in from?"
- Planning communications, change, or governance for a project.

## When NOT to use this skill
- Drafting the actual communications → [communicating-with-stakeholders](../communicating-with-stakeholders/SKILL.md).
- Managing the change program → [managing-change](../managing-change/SKILL.md).
- Assigning task responsibilities only → the RACI in [planning-projects](../planning-projects/SKILL.md).

## Inputs
- The initiative and its scope/impact; who it touches (internal + external).
- What you need from stakeholders (approval, resources, adoption, advocacy).

## Outputs
- A stakeholder map (power/interest grid + attitude), each with needs/concerns, and a
  per-group engagement plan (frequency, channel, message owner).

## Workflow
```
Progress:
- [ ] 1. Identify all stakeholders (affected + influencing, internal + external)
- [ ] 2. Assess each: power, interest, attitude, and what they need
- [ ] 3. Plot on a power/interest grid to set engagement level
- [ ] 4. Identify concerns, influence relationships, and blockers
- [ ] 5. Plan engagement per group (who, what, how often)
- [ ] 6. Review and update as the initiative evolves
```

**Step 3 — power/interest grid** sets the strategy: **high power + high interest** =
manage closely; **high power + low interest** = keep satisfied; **low power + high
interest** = keep informed; **low power + low interest** = monitor. Effort follows
the grid, not equal attention to all.

**Step 4 — attitude and influence.** Note supporters, neutrals, and blockers, and who
influences whom — a low-power stakeholder who advises a decision-maker matters. **Step
6 — stakeholders change;** analysis is living, not one-time.

## Principles
1. **Effort proportional to power × interest.** Don't treat everyone equally.
2. **Understand needs, not just names.** Engagement targets what they care about.
3. **Track attitude and influence networks,** not just org position.
4. **Engage early,** before resistance hardens.
5. **Keep it living** — reassess as the initiative and people shift.

## Decision framework
- **High power, against?** Priority: understand and address concerns directly.
- **High interest, low power, supportive?** Recruit as advocates.
- **Influencer behind a decision-maker?** Engage them to reach the decision-maker.
- **Many similar stakeholders?** Group and engage by segment.

## Common mistakes
- **Listing names without analysis** — no power/interest/attitude assessment.
- **Equal effort to all** — under-managing the critical few.
- **Ignoring informal influence** — mapping only the org chart.
- **One-time analysis** — not updating as things change.
- **Managing supporters, neglecting blockers.**

## Validation checklist
- [ ] All affected and influencing stakeholders identified (incl. external).
- [ ] Each assessed for power, interest, attitude, and needs.
- [ ] Plotted on a power/interest grid; engagement level set.
- [ ] Concerns, blockers, and influence relationships captured.
- [ ] Per-group engagement plan (who/what/how often) defined.
- [ ] Plan owned and scheduled for review.

## Edge cases
- **Hidden/informal stakeholders:** the real decision may sit off the org chart — probe.
- **Coalitions/conflicts:** map alliances and opposing interests explicitly.
- **External regulators/community:** include; their power is often high, interest episodic.
- **Sensitive attitudes:** keep the candid assessment confidential.

## Examples
**Input:** "Stakeholder analysis for rolling out a new CRM."
**Output:** Grid placing the Sales VP (high power/high interest, neutral → manage
closely, address data-migration fear), reps (high interest/low power, mixed → keep
informed, recruit champions), IT (high power/med interest → keep satisfied), and
Finance (approver → keep satisfied); per-group engagement plan with owners and cadence.
