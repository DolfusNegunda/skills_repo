# Managing Change

## Scope
Leading the people side of change so a new system, process, or structure is actually
adopted: building the case, engaging stakeholders, communicating, enabling, and
reinforcing. The human counterpart to the technical rollout in
[planning-projects](../planning-projects/SKILL.md).

## Purpose
Close the gap between "deployed" and "adopted." Most change failures are people, not
technology — this plans the awareness, desire, ability, and reinforcement that make
change stick.

## When to use this skill
- "Manage this change / plan a rollout or adoption / help people adopt X."
- Reducing resistance to a new system, process, or reorg.
- Any initiative whose success depends on people changing behavior.

## When NOT to use this skill
- The technical/delivery plan → [planning-projects](../planning-projects/SKILL.md).
- Stakeholder mapping only → [analyzing-stakeholders](../analyzing-stakeholders/SKILL.md).
- The communications content → [communicating-with-stakeholders](../communicating-with-stakeholders/SKILL.md).

## Inputs
- The change, who it affects and how, and the current vs. desired behavior.
- Stakeholder analysis, sponsors, and known sources of resistance.

## Outputs
- A change plan: the case for change, stakeholder engagement, a communication plan,
  training/enablement, resistance management, and reinforcement/adoption measures.

## Workflow
```
Progress:
- [ ] 1. Define the change and the behavior shift required
- [ ] 2. Build the case for change (the "why", the urgency)
- [ ] 3. Secure active, visible sponsorship
- [ ] 4. Engage stakeholders; surface and address resistance
- [ ] 5. Communicate repeatedly; train/enable people
- [ ] 6. Reinforce: measure adoption, celebrate, correct, sustain
```

**Step 2 — the "why" first (ADKAR: Awareness, Desire).** People resist change they
don't understand the reason for. Make the case and the urgency clear. **Step 3 —
sponsorship is the #1 success factor;** a visible, active sponsor beats any comms
plan. **Step 4 — resistance is data, not defiance;** surface it, understand the root
(fear, workload, loss), and address it. **Step 6 — reinforcement:** without measuring
adoption and reinforcing new behavior, people revert. Deployment is the start, not
the finish.

## Principles
1. **Adoption, not deployment,** is the goal.
2. **Lead with the "why"** — awareness and desire precede ability.
3. **Active sponsorship** is the top predictor of success.
4. **Resistance is information** — engage it, don't suppress it.
5. **Communicate more than feels necessary,** through trusted voices.
6. **Reinforce and measure** — change reverts without it.

## Decision framework
- **Large/disruptive change?** Full model (case, sponsor coalition, phased rollout, reinforcement).
- **High resistance expected?** Front-load engagement and two-way listening.
- **Distributed workforce?** Multi-channel comms + local champions.
- **Behavior-critical?** Emphasize training and reinforcement over announcements.

## Common mistakes
- **Treating go-live as the finish** — no reinforcement, people revert.
- **Announcing, not engaging** — one-way comms, no listening.
- **Weak/invisible sponsorship.**
- **Ignoring or dismissing resistance.**
- **Under-communicating** — assuming once is enough.
- **No adoption measurement** — "done" with no evidence anyone changed.

## Validation checklist
- [ ] Required behavior change is defined.
- [ ] A clear, compelling case for change ("why") exists.
- [ ] An active, visible sponsor is secured.
- [ ] Stakeholders engaged; resistance surfaced and addressed.
- [ ] Communication plan (repeated, multi-channel) and training in place.
- [ ] Adoption measured; reinforcement mechanisms defined.

## Edge cases
- **Change fatigue:** acknowledge prior changes; connect this one to relief, not more burden.
- **Layoffs/reorg:** handle with transparency and empathy; rumor fills silence.
- **Mandated/regulatory change:** still needs the "why" and enablement, not just a deadline.
- **Cultural change:** slower; requires leadership modeling and sustained reinforcement.

## Examples
**Input:** "Plan adoption for our new CRM going live in 8 weeks."
**Output:** Change plan: case for change (why the old tool costs deals), Sales VP as
active sponsor, champion network per region, a comms drumbeat (why → what → how →
support), role-based training before go-live, a resistance plan addressing "it's
slower at first", and adoption metrics (login rate, records created, pipeline
accuracy) with reinforcement at 30/60/90 days.

## Automation opportunities
- Track adoption metrics automatically and trigger reinforcement when they dip.
- Reuse the change-plan structure as an org template for rollouts.
