# Analytical Report Structure

```
Title
Author · Date · Audience · Status

1. Executive Summary
   - The answer + key findings + top recommendations. Standalone.

2. Background & Scope
   - Why this report exists, the question it answers, what's in/out of scope.

3. Method
   - How the analysis was done; data sources; limitations. (Brief.)

4. Findings                          [FACTS ONLY]
   - What the data/investigation shows, with evidence.
   - Charts/tables labeled; each finding a clear statement.

5. Analysis                          [INTERPRETATION]
   - What the findings mean: implications, causes, patterns, uncertainty.
   - Kept separate from Section 4 so the reader can judge the reasoning.

6. Recommendations                   [ACTIONS]
   | # | Recommendation | Owner | Priority | Expected impact |
   |---|----------------|-------|----------|-----------------|

7. Next Steps / Timeline

Appendices
   A. Full data tables
   B. Detailed methodology
   C. Supporting evidence
```

Rules: answer first; facts ≠ interpretation ≠ recommendations; every body claim
traces to evidence.
