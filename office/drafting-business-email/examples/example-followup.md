# Worked Example: Chasing a Follow-Up

## Input (user's request)

> "Chase Daniel at Northgate — I sent him the signed SOW two weeks ago and
> asked for the kickoff date, heard nothing. We can't book the engineers until
> he confirms. Don't want to sound annoyed, he's usually good."

Extracted brief:
- Recipient: Daniel (external client, Northgate), normally responsive
- Ask: confirm the kickoff date
- Stakes: engineer scheduling is blocked
- Round: second follow-up (original sent two weeks ago) → firmer than a first
  nudge, but relationship is good, so lead with good faith

## Recommended draft — professional-neutral, ~75 words

```
Subject: Northgate SOW — kickoff date needed this week

Hi Daniel,

Following up on the signed SOW I sent on 24 June — I haven't seen a kickoff
date come back yet.

We're holding engineering capacity for this project, and I can keep that hold
until Friday. Could you confirm a kickoff week by then, even provisionally?

If someone else at Northgate now owns scheduling, happy to pick this up with
them directly.

Best regards,
[Name]
```

Why it works: the subject carries the deadline; the ask lands in sentence two;
the consequence (capacity hold expiring) creates urgency without accusation;
the redirect offer gives Daniel an easy out if he's not the blocker.

## Variant A — warmer, ~65 words

For when the relationship matters more than the deadline pressure.

```
Subject: Northgate kickoff — can we lock a date?

Hi Daniel,

Hope things are good on your side. I know how the start of quarter gets —
just making sure the SOW I sent on 24 June didn't get buried.

We'd love to lock in a kickoff week so I can keep the engineering team
reserved for you. Would a quick reply with a rough week work, even if the
exact date moves later?

Thanks,
[Name]
```

## Variant B — concise and firm, ~45 words

For a third follow-up, or when the schedule risk now outweighs politeness.

```
Subject: Northgate kickoff — confirm by Fri 11 Jul or we release the hold

Daniel,

Third time asking on this one. The SOW has been signed since 24 June and we
still don't have a kickoff date.

We're holding engineers for Northgate through Friday 11 July. Without a
confirmed date by then, that capacity goes to the next project.

Best regards,
[Name]
```

## Fill-ins flagged to the user

- `[Name]` — sender's sign-off name
- Dates (24 June, Friday 11 July) were inferred from "two weeks ago" — confirm
  before sending
