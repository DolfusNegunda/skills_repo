# Reviewing Books

## Scope
Developmental-level review of book-length work — manuscripts, chapters, long
reports — for structure, argument/story, craft, and reader experience. Inherits
method/severity/scoring from
[conducting-structured-reviews](../conducting-structured-reviews/SKILL.md). Line-
level polish is [editing-prose](../../office/editing-prose/SKILL.md).

## Purpose
Give an author a developmental critique: does the whole work hold together, does it
serve its reader, and what are the highest-leverage revisions — before line-editing
or publishing.

## When to use this skill
- "Review my book / manuscript / chapter / long-form draft."
- "Developmental edit / big-picture feedback" on a long work.
- Assessing a book's readiness before publishing or querying.

## When NOT to use this skill
- Line editing/polish → [editing-prose](../../office/editing-prose/SKILL.md).
- Proofreading → [proofreading-text](../../office/proofreading-text/SKILL.md).
- Short documents → [reviewing-documents](../reviewing-documents/SKILL.md).
- Academic rigor → [reviewing-research](../reviewing-research/SKILL.md).

## Inputs
- The manuscript (or a substantial sample) and its genre/category.
- Intended audience, the author's goal, and comparable titles if relevant.

## Outputs
- A developmental review: overall verdict + scores, severity-ranked findings at the
  structural level, chapter-level notes, and genuine strengths.

## Evaluation rubric (dimensions)
1. **Structure** — logical/narrative architecture; chapters earn their place and order.
2. **Argument (non-fiction) / Story (fiction)** — thesis holds and builds / plot, stakes, arc cohere.
3. **Audience fit** — level, tone, and assumed knowledge match the intended reader.
4. **Pacing** — momentum sustained; no sag, no rush; scene/section length balanced.
5. **Clarity & craft** — prose serves the content; voice consistent; devices land.
6. **Evidence/consistency** — non-fiction claims supported; fiction internally consistent (characters, world, timeline).
7. **Openings & payoff** — a hook that earns attention; a satisfying, on-promise ending.

## Scoring & severity
Score 1–5; rank Blocker→Praise (see
[foundation reference](../conducting-structured-reviews/references/severity-and-scoring.md)).
Examples: **Blocker** = the central argument doesn't hold / the plot has a structural
hole; **Major** = a 60-page mid-section sag; **Minor** = one weak chapter opening;
**Nit** = an overused phrase.

## Workflow
```
Progress:
- [ ] 1. Confirm genre, audience, and the author's goal
- [ ] 2. Read for the whole: does the structure/arc work?
- [ ] 3. Assess argument/story, pacing, and audience fit
- [ ] 4. Note chapter-level issues and consistency
- [ ] 5. Severity-rank, score, verdict; specific revisions + strengths
```

**Step 2 — structure first, always.** Developmental review is about the architecture,
not commas; a manuscript with a broken structure isn't ready for line edits. **Be
constructive** — long-form work represents huge effort; findings must be honest but
actionable and paired with what works.

## Recommended-improvements guidance
Give the structural move: reorder/cut/merge chapters, strengthen the through-line,
raise the stakes, tighten the sagging middle, or rewrite the opening to hook. Point
to specific chapters/sections, not "the middle drags" alone.

## Validation checklist
- [ ] Genre, audience, and author goal confirmed.
- [ ] Structure and argument/story assessed at the whole-work level.
- [ ] Pacing, audience fit, and consistency evaluated.
- [ ] Findings carry location (chapter/section) + severity + a revision.
- [ ] Verdict + scores given; genuine strengths acknowledged.
- [ ] Tone constructive and about the work.

## Edge cases
- **Sample/partial manuscript:** review what's present; flag that whole-arc judgment is limited.
- **Fiction vs. non-fiction:** swap "argument" for "story" and apply the matching craft lens.
- **Series:** assess this volume standalone and its role in the arc.
- **Self-published/indie:** include market/positioning fit if the author wants it.

## Examples
**Input:** "Developmental review of my non-fiction manuscript on remote work."
**Output:** Verdict: Revise (Structure 2/5, Argument 3/5). **Blocker:** chapters 2–4
each restate the same thesis without advancing it — the argument doesn't build;
fix: merge into one and let later chapters extend it. **Major:** the middle third
lacks examples and drags. **Praise:** a compelling, concrete opening chapter.

## Automation opportunities
- Use the rubric as a pre-submission developmental checklist.
- Chain into [editing-prose](../../office/editing-prose/SKILL.md) once structure is fixed.
